## üéì Interview Tips & Best Practices

### DO:
- ‚úÖ Listen more than talk (80/20 rule)
- ‚úÖ Ask "why" and "tell me more" frequently
- ‚úÖ Let silence happen (people fill it with insights)
- ‚úÖ Ask for specific examples ("tell me about last time...")
- ‚úÖ Dig into pain points (quantify time/cost)
- ‚úÖ Record (with permission) for accurate quotes
- ‚úÖ Send thank-you + compensation within 24h

### DON'T:
- ‚ùå Pitch your product (this is discovery, not sales)
- ‚ùå Ask leading questions ("Don't you think X is a problem?")
- ‚ùå Defend your solution if criticized
- ‚ùå Skip the "current state" questions
- ‚ùå Rush through pricing questions
- ‚ùå Forget to ask for referrals
- ‚ùå Overpromise ("We'll build feature X for you!")

### Red Flags to Watch For:
- üö© "Yeah, that could be nice" (lukewarm interest = no)
- üö© Can't quantify pain ("it's a bit annoying" = not urgent)
- üö© "We'd need to check with [boss/team/IT]" (not decision maker)
- üö© "We're building something similar internally" (not a customer)
- üö© "Sounds interesting, send me info" (polite rejection)

### Green Flags to Look For:
- ‚úÖ Quantifies pain ("we waste 15 hours/week on this")
- ‚úÖ Already looking for solution ("we tried X, Y, Z")
- ‚úÖ Decision maker or strong influence
- ‚úÖ Excited to be beta tester ("when can I start?")
- ‚úÖ Asks detailed questions about how it works
- ‚úÖ Offers to refer colleagues
